In a blend of engineering precision and musical artistry, our project embarked on the design and fabrication of a tongue drum, a percussive musical instrument renowned for its melodious and harmonic tones. This journey, documented in our Bachelor Thesis at Boğaziçi University's Department of Mechanical Engineering, was a pursuit of harmonizing scientific principles with the art of music making. Let's look at the beautiful tongue drums together from a scientific perspective.

The tongue drum, deriving its name from the tongue-shaped slits that produce its unique sounds, is an idiophone that resonates through the vibration of its body. Drawing inspiration from various traditional instruments and incorporating a scientific approach, we aimed to refine and innovate upon the traditional tongue drum design.

## The Genesis of Sound
Our project began with an in-depth exploration of existing tongue drum designs, from the materials used to the acoustic principles governing their sound production. The decision to design a hybrid tongue drum was motivated by the desire to combine the rich, resonant qualities of metal tongues with the warm, natural tones of a wooden body. Through iterative design and finite element analysis, we examined the influence of materials, tongue shapes, and the drum's acoustic cavity on its sound.

## Material Matters
The choice of materials played a pivotal role in our design process. After considering various options, we opted for a combination of mulberry wood for the top plate and Medium-Density Fibreboard (MDF) for the body. Mulberry wood was chosen for its density and elastic properties, contributing to the drum's tonal qualities. The material properties of the mulberry wood, crucial for tuning the tongues to precise frequencies, were determined through experimental methods.

## Design and Analysis
Our design iterations were accompanied by detailed analysis using ANSYS software, where we simulated structural and acoustic behaviors of the tongue drum. This analysis helped refine our understanding of how different design parameters affected the drum's sound. After several iterations, we settled on a design that aligned with our goal of creating a harmonically rich and aesthetically pleasing musical instrument.

## Crafting Harmonies
The manufacturing process involved precision cutting of the mulberry wood top plate using a CNC machine to create the tongues, ensuring accuracy in dimensions critical for achieving the desired musical notes. The assembly emphasized airtight construction to enhance acoustic performance, with special attention to eliminating potential rattling and ensuring stability of the structure.

## Tuning and Testing
The final stage involved tuning the tongues and testing the drum's sound. Despite the meticulous design and analysis, the experimental frequencies of some tongues deviated from our theoretical predictions, highlighting the challenges of translating theoretical models into physical artifacts. This discrepancy prompted further analysis and adjustments to our approach, underscoring the iterative nature of engineering design.

## Conclusion
Our project, a convergence of engineering and music, not only culminated in the creation of a unique musical instrument but also enriched our understanding of the complex interplay between material science, acoustics, and craftsmanship. The journey underscored the importance of embracing both analytical rigor and creative experimentation in the pursuit of innovation.
